this blog is about how i don't waste my life with a life plan. the philosphy is simple.

1. we all want to be significant in the eyes of others
2. the surest way to be significant is to serve others
3. service comes from ethical living

sounds simple, right? but if you look around at your life or that of your peers—do they abide by these principles? or are they driven by something else?

i believe that no amount of money, skill, achievement will fill the hole inside of us.a hole that craves connection and wants to be part of something bigger than themselves. yet when we talk about 'not wasting your life,' we tend to answer the question with 'make your dent in the universe,' or an answer that is similarly individualistic and ambitious.

i don't believe the purusit of achievement, in itself, will ever bring you peace. this is not a new idea, and is recurring theme in [ecclesiates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecclesiastes). king solomon, the man who has everything, comes to the understanding that seeking fulfillments in 'wordly pursuits,' is like chasing the wind: what you want is always out of your grasp. is that what not wasting your life is? chasing the wind?
